# 🌌 THOTH: System Initialization & User Tutorial

Welcome to the **THOTH Space Weather & Climate Command Center**! This system is designed for real-time monitoring of planetary atmospheric telemetry, space weather index metrics, and geomagnetic variations. 

This guide will explain how to operate the command console and leverage THOTH's scientific sensors.

## 1. What is THOTH?
THOTH is an advanced interactive WebGL application. By unifying data streams from USGS, NASA EONET, and NOAA Space Weather predictions, THOTH visualizes the invisible forces of our planet—from tropospheric climate cycles and major hurricanes to ionospheric stability and Schumann Resonance patterns.

## 2. Navigating the Command Console

The application interface is divided into three main operational zones:

### ⬅️ The Left Panel: Atmospheric & Meteorological Intelligence
*   **Active Climate Advisories**: Real-time alerts regarding solar storms, extreme heat waves, geomagnetic fluctuations, or localized storms.
*   **Meteorological Assessment**: AI-generated, high-fidelity regional weather briefs and forecasts. Contains interactive, encrypted-style telemetry readouts.
*   **Space Weather Live Telemetry**: Live satellite visual feeds, tracking coordinates, and auroral activity flags.

### 🌍 The Center Stage: WebGL Telemetry Globe
*   **Planetary Globe**: Click and drag to rotate the Earth; scroll to zoom. The globe maps environmental events as glowing status indicators.
*   **Atmospheric Filters**: Toggle data layers above the globe to view space weather nodes, meteorological flights, or switch to a flat 2D projection.
*   **Active Satellite Tracking**: Visualizes weather satellites orbiting the Earth along their predicted trajectories.

### ➡️ The Right Panel: Climatology Sensors & Schumann Resonance
*   **Atmospheric Sensors**: Real-time metrics for **Ambient Temperature (TEMP)**, **Relative Humidity (HUMID)**, and **Wind Velocity (WIND)**.
*   **12-Month Climatology Chart**: Dynamic seasonal graphs illustrating climate trends based on regional observation data.
*   **Schumann Resonance Monitor**: Visualizes the Earth-ionosphere cavity resonance frequencies (1.0 to 40.0 Hz) with an active waterfall plot.

## 3. Core Features & Operations

### 🛰️ Feature 1: Analyze Regional Weather Telemetry
1. Rotate the globe to locate a country of interest, or use the search bar.
2. Click on the country to slide open its telemetry dossier.
3. Review the **Overview** for immediate climate advisories, the **Atmosphere** tab for radar propagation stability, or **Climatology** for seasonal patterns.

### 🌊 Feature 2: Track the Schumann Resonance
1. Access the **Schumann Resonance** visualizer on the console.
2. View the real-time canvas-rendered **Waterfall Plot** capturing electromagnetic resonance peaks (from the fundamental 7.83 Hz up to 40.0 Hz).
3. Monitor shifts in frequency amplitude, which signal geomagnetic disturbances or solar wind impacts.

### 🚀 Feature 3: Monitor Satellite Trajectories
1. Watch the orbiting satellite icons navigate the globe's atmosphere in real-time.
2. Track coordinates and sensor footprints directly from the globe's orbital paths.
